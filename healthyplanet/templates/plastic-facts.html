1. In 2020, 367 million tonnes of plastic were produced

Global plastic production has increased almost every year since the 1950s.

Source: Our World In Data
2. Every year, 500 billion plastic bottles are created

To put that in context, if you stacked 500 billion small coke bottles on top of each other, your bottle tower would reach two thirds of the way to the sun!

Source: Statista

3. Some plastics can take up to 500 years to decompose

A disposable plastic toothbrush that's thrown away in 2022 might not decompose until the year 2522.

Source: WWF
4. About 55 percent of all the plastic ever produced has been thrown away

An estimated 4.6 billion tonnes of plastic has ended up in landfills, discarded in the environment or dumped in the sea.

Source: Our World In Data

5. The Great Pacific Garbage Patch covers 1.6 million square kilometers in the Pacific Ocean

It's three times the size of France and is just one of five huge collections of rubbish that have formed from the 8 million tonnes of plastic that ends up in our oceans each year.

Source: The Ocean Cleanup
6. Packaging accounts for 141 million tonnes of plastic waste per year

Packaging is by far the biggest contributor to plastic waste each year. Plastic packaging is usually used for 6 months or less before being thrown away.

Source: Our World In Data, data from 2015

7. By 2050, the oceans could contain more plastic than fish

If current trends continue, there could be 1 tonne of plastic for every 3 tonnes of fish by 2025, and more plastic than fish by 2050.

Source: World Economic Forum
8. Most of the plastic that's been dumped into the ocean is missing

We can see more than 250,000 tonnes of waste plastic in the ocean, but we know that 10 million tonnes ends up in the ocean each year. Scientists aren't sure what's happened to the missing plastic.

Source: Physics World
